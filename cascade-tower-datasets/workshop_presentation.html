BEFORE YOU START

Prompting Tips for Great Results

🎯

Give Context First

"Here is earned value data for an $82M construction project" is much better than "make a chart from this data."

📋

Be Specific About Output

Use numbered lists: "Build an HTML dashboard with: 1) a bar chart of X, 2) a line chart of Y, 3) a table of Z."

🔁

Iterate, Don't Start Over

"Make the chart bigger" · "Add a red line at 1.00" · "Use UBC colours." Each round improves the result.

🐛

Paste Errors Back

If it's broken, describe what's wrong or paste the error message. AI is very good at fixing its own output.

Three Takeaways

🧠

Domain Expertise is Your Edge

AI generates code and charts. It cannot replace your understanding of construction logic, contracts, and project dynamics. You are the quality filter.

⚡

AI Augments, Not Replaces

AI won't replace project managers. But PMs who use AI will outperform those who don't. It's a tool — like BIM was 15 years ago.

🎓

Start Experimenting Now

The best time to build AI fluency is while you're in school — low stakes, high learning. Push what you built today further.

The Project: Cascade Tower

All five datasets describe the same fictional project — a 25-storey mixed-use tower at 1200 W Georgia St, Vancouver. $82M contract value, 18-month schedule, currently at Month 15. A major facade supplier failure in Month 6 caused a 21-day critical path delay — visible across all datasets.

Your Datasets & Starter Prompts

Download your assigned CSV. Review the columns. Then paste its contents into your AI tool of choice (Claude, ChatGPT, Gemini — any works).

Student A — Schedule Performance

42 activities with planned vs actual dates, critical path, float, delays. Build a visual schedule analysis showing where the project is behind and why.

Here is construction schedule data for Cascade Tower, a 25-storey mixed-use project in Vancouver ($82M, 18 months). The data has 42 activities with planned and actual dates, critical path status, delays, and float. Build me an interactive HTML dashboard with: 1. A horizontal bar chart showing planned vs actual duration for each activity, colour-coded by phase 2. A table of all delayed activities sorted by delay days (highest first), with the delay reason 3. A summary showing: total critical path delay, number of activities complete vs in progress vs not started 4. A phase-by-phase breakdown showing average delay per phase Use a clean, modern design. Make charts interactive with hover tooltips.

Student B — Earned Value & Cost

18 months of EVM data (BCWS, BCWP, ACWP, SPI, CPI, EAC). Build S-curves, performance trends, and forecasting.

Here is 15 months of earned value data for Cascade Tower, an $82M construction project in Vancouver. Months 16-18 are planned only (no actuals yet). Build me an interactive HTML dashboard with: 1. KPI summary cards showing BAC, Earned Value to date, Actual Cost, SPI, CPI, and EAC 2. A cumulative S-curve chart plotting BCWS, BCWP, and ACWP over time 3. SPI and CPI trend lines with a target line at 1.00 — colour code: green above 1.00, amber 0.90-0.99, red below 0.90 4. Monthly schedule variance and cost variance as a grouped bar chart 5. EAC trend line showing how the forecast has changed over time vs the original BAC 6. Below each chart, add a short explanation of what it means and what the key takeaway is for this project Use a modern dark theme. Make everything interactive with hover tooltips.

Student C — Safety Incidents

27 incidents and near misses over 15 months. Severity, trade, weather, root causes, corrective actions.

Here is a safety incident log for Cascade Tower, a 25-storey construction project in Vancouver. It has 27 incidents over 15 months including near misses, injuries, and environmental events. Build me an interactive HTML dashboard with: 1. Monthly incident frequency chart (bar or line) with a trend line 2. A severity breakdown — pie or donut chart showing distribution across severity levels 1-4 3. A heat map or grouped bar chart showing incidents by trade/subcontractor 4. A summary of: total incidents, near misses, WorkSafeBC-reported incidents, total lost-time days 5. A table of the most severe incidents (severity 3+) with date, description, and corrective action 6. An incident type breakdown (fall, struck by, electrical, etc.) Use UBC colours (blue #002145, gold #F2A900). Make charts interactive.

Student D — Risk Register

15 project risks with likelihood/impact scores, cost exposure, mitigation strategies, and status.

Here is a project risk register for Cascade Tower, a 25-storey construction project in Vancouver ($82M). It has 15 risks across categories like geotechnical, supply chain, safety, financial, and commissioning. Build me an interactive HTML dashboard with: 1. A 5x5 likelihood vs impact risk matrix (heat map) — plot each risk as a dot, colour by status 2. A bar chart of total cost exposure by risk category 3. A status breakdown showing how many risks are Active, Mitigated, Realized, or Closed 4. The top 5 highest-scoring risks in a detailed table with title, score, owner, and current status 5. A summary showing: total number of risks, total cost exposure, number of realized risks, and total schedule exposure days 6. For realized risks, show what actually happened vs what was planned Make it interactive with clickable/hoverable elements. Use a professional colour scheme.

Student E — BIM Clash Detection

42 clash results with discipline pairs, severity, resolution method, cost impact, timeline.

Here is a BIM clash detection report from Cascade Tower, a 25-storey construction project in Vancouver. It contains 42 clashes detected during BIM coordination, with details on disciplines, elements, severity, resolution method, and cost impact. Build me an interactive HTML dashboard with: 1. A discipline-pair matrix or chord diagram showing which discipline combinations produce the most clashes 2. A breakdown of clashes by priority (Critical/Major/Minor) and status (Resolved/Open) 3. Total cost impact by discipline — bar chart showing which trades are most expensive to resolve 4. Average days to resolve by priority level 5. A timeline chart showing when clashes were detected and resolved over the project timeline 6. A table of all open/unresolved clashes with their details Use a modern design. Make charts interactive with hover tooltips.

Prompting Tips

🎯 Give Context First

Tell the AI what the data is, what the project is, and who the audience is. The prompts above already do this — add more if needed.

📋 Be Specific About Charts

Don't just say "make a dashboard." List exactly what charts, metrics, and format you want. Numbered lists work best.

🔁 Iterate and Refine

Your first result won't be perfect — that's the point. Say "make the chart bigger", "add red highlighting", "use UBC colours."

🐛 Paste Errors Back

If the HTML doesn't render, describe the problem. AI is excellent at debugging its own code.

💡 Try Different AI Tools

Claude, ChatGPT, and Gemini all have strengths. If one gives mediocre results, try another.

🎨 Ask for Explanations

Add "explain each chart" to your prompt. The AI will add insight boxes that help you and your audience understand the data.

Good luck! You have 25 minutes. Raise your hand if you get stuck.
